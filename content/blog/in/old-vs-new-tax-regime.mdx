# Old vs New Tax Regime: Which is Better for Your Salary?

The Union Budget 2025 brought significant changes to the New Tax Regime, making it even more attractive. But does that mean the Old Regime is dead? Not necessarily. Let's break it down.

## The New Regime (Default)

The New Regime is now the **default** for all taxpayers. Here are the updated slabs for FY 2025-26:

| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹4,00,000 | Nil |
| ₹4,00,001 – ₹8,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹8,00,001 – ₹12,00,000 | 10% |
| ₹12,00,001 – ₹16,00,000 | 15% |
| ₹16,00,001 – ₹20,00,000 | 20% |
| ₹20,00,001 – ₹24,00,000 | 25% |
| Above ₹24,00,000 | 30% |

**Key benefit:** Standard deduction of ₹75,000 and full rebate under Section 87A for taxable income up to ₹12,00,000.

## The Old Regime

The Old Regime allows you to claim deductions under sections like 80C, 80D, HRA, and LTA. Here are the slabs:

| Income Slab | Tax Rate |
|---|---|
| Up to ₹2,50,000 | Nil |
| ₹2,50,001 – ₹5,00,000 | 5% |
| ₹5,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 | 20% |
| Above ₹10,00,000 | 30% |

## When is the Old Regime Better?

The Old Regime is better **only** if your total deductions exceed approximately ₹3.75 lakh. This typically applies to people who:

- Have a home loan (Section 24 interest deduction up to ₹2L)
- Pay significant rent in metro cities (HRA exemption)
- Invest heavily in PPF, ELSS, NPS (Section 80C + 80CCD)
- Have health insurance for self and parents (Section 80D)

## Quick Decision Matrix

| Your CTC | Deductions < ₹2L | Deductions ₹2L-₹3.75L | Deductions > ₹3.75L |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 12 LPA | New ✅ | New ✅ | Old ✅ |
| 12-20 LPA | New ✅ | Depends 🤔 | Old ✅ |
| > 20 LPA | New ✅ | Depends 🤔 | Old ✅ |
