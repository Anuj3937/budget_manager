# Section 80C: The Ultimate Deduction Guide

Section 80C of the Income Tax Act is the most popular tax-saving section for salaried individuals in India. It allows deductions up to **₹1,50,000** per financial year.

## Eligible Investments Under 80C

### 1. Employee Provident Fund (EPF)
Your EPF contribution (12% of basic salary) automatically qualifies. **Most salaried employees already use ₹50,000-₹80,000 of their 80C limit through EPF alone.**

### 2. Public Provident Fund (PPF)
- Lock-in: 15 years
- Interest Rate: ~7.1% (tax-free)
- Max Investment: ₹1.5L per year
- Best for: Conservative, long-term investors

### 3. ELSS Mutual Funds
- Lock-in: **Just 3 years** (shortest among 80C options)
- Returns: 10-15% CAGR historically
- Best for: Growth-oriented investors
- **Our recommendation for young professionals**

### 4. Life Insurance Premiums
- Premiums paid towards life insurance policies qualify
- Avoid investment-cum-insurance plans (ULIPs with high charges)
- Prefer term insurance instead

### 5. Others
- National Savings Certificate (NSC)
- 5-year Tax-Saving Fixed Deposits
- Tuition fees for children (max 2 children)
- Home loan principal repayment

## The Smart Strategy

For a salaried professional earning 10-20 LPA:

1. **EPF** covers ₹50,000-₹80,000 automatically
2. **ELSS** for the remaining ₹70,000-₹1,00,000 (best returns + shortest lock-in)

**Section 80C deductions are NOT available under the New Tax Regime.** Before investing purely for tax savings, compare both regimes in our [Tax Hub](/tax-hub).
