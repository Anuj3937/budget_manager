# The 50/30/20 Rule: The Simplest Budget That Actually Works

If you've ever felt overwhelmed by complex budgeting spreadsheets, this article is for you. The 50/30/20 rule is the simplest, most effective framework to manage your money — and it works at any income level.

## The Rule

Split your **after-tax income** into three buckets:

| Bucket | Percentage | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Needs** | 50% | Rent, groceries, utilities, insurance, EMIs |
| **Wants** | 30% | Dining out, entertainment, subscriptions, shopping |
| **Savings** | 20% | Emergency fund, investments, debt repayment |

## Real-World Example

**Monthly take-home: ₹80,000 (or $3,500)**

| Bucket | Amount |
|---|---|
| Needs (50%) | ₹40,000 / $1,750 |
| Wants (30%) | ₹24,000 / $1,050 |
| Savings (20%) | ₹16,000 / $700 |

1. **Start with needs and savings first.** Whatever is left becomes your "wants" budget.
2. **Automate your savings.** Set up auto-transfers on payday to remove willpower from the equation.

## When to Modify the Rule

- **High-cost cities (NYC, Mumbai, London):** Adjust to 60/20/20 if rent alone takes 35%+
- **Aggressive savers:** Flip to 50/20/30 (30% savings) if you're targeting early financial independence
- **Debt payoff mode:** Shift to 50/20/30 with 30% going to debt repayment
